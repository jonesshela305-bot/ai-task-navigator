Ask AI to teach you — not just answer you

❌ Don't ask

  • ❌ What's the answer?

✅ Ask instead

  • ✅ Teach me how to solve this.
  • ✅ Explain the steps.
  • ✅ Create a practice quiz.
  • ✅ Give me another example.
  • ✅ Act like my tutor.

Key concept

AI Assistants plan. Automation platforms run.

These are two different types of tools that work best together. Understanding the difference helps you build smarter, faster workflows.

AI Assistant Claude · ChatGPT · Gemini

Plans the automation. Describe your goal and Claude will map out the workflow — which apps to connect, what triggers to use, what actions to set, and what data to pass between steps. Use Claude before you open Zapier.

Automation Platform Zapier · Make · n8n

Runs the automation. These platforms connect your apps and execute the workflow automatically — no manual steps required. They respond to triggers, move data, and take action around the clock.

Example workflow

Ask Claude: "I want to automatically send a follow-up email when someone fills out my contact form and add them to a Google Sheet." Claude designs the workflow and gives you step-by-step Zapier setup instructions. Then you build it in Zapier and it runs automatically from that point on.

No-code and low-code automation platforms that connect apps, trigger workflows, and eliminate repetitive tasks — no programming required.

AI Fluency

The 4D Framework

AI Fluency develops through intentional practice of four core competencies.

D

Delegation

Our expertise and judgment remain the foundation of effective AI collaboration.

D

Description

Clear communication bridges our intentions and AI capabilities.

D

Discernment

Thoughtful and critical evaluation of outputs helps us work within AI limitations.

D

Diligence

Accountability, transparency, and responsibility ensure effective AI use.

The most powerful outcomes emerge when humans and AI build on each other's strengths.

AI Guardrails

Five principles for responsible AI use

These guardrails support the 4D Framework. Apply them every time you use AI for school, work, or business.

Verify Before You Trust

AI can make mistakes. Always verify important information.

Protect Sensitive Information

Do not enter passwords, Social Security numbers, banking information, or confidential data into AI tools.

Human Judgment Comes First

AI assists decision-making and should not replace professional judgment.

Use AI Responsibly

Respect privacy, ethics, workplace policies, and copyright.

Check Sources

Review citations and original references whenever possible.

Mini lesson

The R-C-T-O Prompt Method

A simple structure that makes any AI prompt work better.

R
RoleTell AI who to act as.
C
ContextExplain your situation.
T
TaskTell AI what to do.
O
OutputTell AI how to format the answer.
Next-level lesson

The R-C-T-F-T-C Prompt Method

Once you're comfortable with R-C-T-O, add Format, Tone, and Constraints to get sharper, more usable answers.

R
RoleAct as a coach, teacher, reviewer, strategist, or assistant.
C
ContextShare the background, goal, audience, problem, or assignment.
T
TaskState clearly what you want the AI to do.
F
FormatBullets, table, checklist, email, outline, paragraph, or script.
T
ToneProfessional, friendly, beginner-friendly, academic, or warm.
C
ConstraintsWord count, reading level, no fluff, cite sources, APA/MLA, avoid jargon.
